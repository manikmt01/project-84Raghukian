Online proctored exams are secure, timed evaluations used for academic and employment purposes. They take place on secure exam platforms that use the test-taker's webcam and audio to monitor screen activity and prevent cheating. The recordings are then reviewed by human invigilators and examiners who evaluate the answers once the test is completed and submitted.

  • Unbiased assessments
  • Continuous and error-free supervision
  • Greater time efficiency
  • Cost-effectiveness
  • Scalability to run exams simultaneously in multiple places
  • Possibility of scheduling multiple candidates (even those in far-off places) to take the test on the same day
  • Administration and assessment are simple
  • Better opportunities for customization and coordination
  • Flexibility in response to various question kinds and exam formats
  • Characteristics for 24/7 exam scheduling

Typically, an online proctored exam starts with having test-takers show the four walls of their room as an anti-cheat measure. Then, they undergo identity verification, audio and camera testing, screen sharing and must ensure there are no other devices present in the room. The proctoring software then monitors the test-taker's screen activity, locks the web browsers, and allows for flagging of any suspicious activity to prevent cheating.

The moment the room scan is completed, the student's original photo, Identity card photos and room scan videos are updated and can be seen by the proctor and the professor. The moment the auto exam is over, the exam recordings (webcam videos and screen recordings) are uploaded and can be viewed. In case of a live exam, only the violation videos and flagged videos (webcam videos and screen recordings) are uploaded after the exam is over.

A proctored exam uses AI technology to monitor the test-taker's screen activity or is monitored by a remote human invigilator. On the other hand, non-proctored exams are unmonitored and allow the test-taker to take the test at their own pace. Proctored exams offer more reliability and fairness in the testing and evaluation process compared to non-proctored exams.

Professors from the institute have the ability to create exams for their subjects and assess the exam sessions once they have been reviewed by a proctor. The proctor, who can either be an appointed NthEye individual or a trained representative provided by the institute, is responsible for reviewing exam sessions and monitoring live exams to ensure the integrity of the examination process.

The proctors are equipped to handle flagged videos during the review process, determining whether they are a violation of exam rules or evidence of cheating. If the flagged video is found to not be cheating, the proctor will unflag it. To maintain fairness, the professor has the final say in unflagging videos that were flagged for non-cheating reasons.
